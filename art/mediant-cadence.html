Mediant Cadence

tree → line → plane → time. every positive rational, as a melody.

Every positive rational p/q sits at a unique address in the Stern–Brocot tree — a finite sequence of Ls and Rs. Read that sequence as a walk on a pentatonic ladder: L steps down, R steps up. Each rational becomes a melody, and the famous limits become rhythms — φ is the perfect zigzag, π is bursts then long runs. The run-lengths of the path are the continued fraction. Sound on.

Three earlier siblings — tree, line, plane — are the same arithmetic seen in space. This one is the same arithmetic seen in time.
