Golden Slow

Every irrational can be chased by fractions. Some surrender quickly — π halts at 355/113, seven correct digits for a denominator of three. Some never surrender. The golden ratio φ is the slowest fugitive of all: at every step, its continued fraction whispers the smallest possible integer — 1 — and the chase barely closes. Below, five constants race their best rational approximations against the Hurwitz floor, the theoretical limit no irrational can outrun. Only φ stays glued to it.

The vertical axis is the number of correct decimal digits in the convergent p/q. The horizontal axis is log₁₀ of the denominator q — the cost of the approximation. The diagonal dashed line is the Hurwitz floor: no irrational α admits infinitely many p/q with |α − p/q| < 1/(√5·q²). The constant √5 is sharp, achieved exactly by φ and its Markov siblings.

Continued-fraction sources: π → A001203, e → A003417, √2 → A040000, γ → A002852. For φ every term is 1 — which is exactly why it cannot be approximated efficiently. To be the most golden, in this sense, is to be the most stubborn.
