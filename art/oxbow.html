Oxbow — a river that forgets

meander migration, cutoff, abandonment — drawn live

step 0 channel pts 0 cutoffs 0

Rivers don't flow straight. The outside of a bend erodes; the inside collects sediment. Every meander leans more over time — until two parts of the channel touch. Then the river takes the shortcut. The old loop is abandoned: cut off, becoming an oxbow lake, then a damp scar, then nothing.

The pale ghosts here are channels the river used to be. The bright thread is what it is now. Watch long enough and the bright thread becomes a ghost too.
