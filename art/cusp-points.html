Cusp Points

the residual set — where the circles never reach, only approach

0 circles · 0 cusps

Take an Apollonian gasket — four mutually tangent circles, then their reflections, then theirs, forever. The circles tile the disk. They are production: easy, visible, integer-curvatured, arriving by a clean rule.

Now ignore the circles. Look only at the cusps — the points where each pair of tangent circles meets. Those points are not on any circle interior. They are the limit set. They are where the gasket actually lives, as a fractal. They are comprehension: the residual, the dense-but-measure-zero leftover, the place where infinite refinement is required to land.

Watch production tile the plane. Watch its complement emerge.
