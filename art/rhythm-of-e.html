The Rhythm of e

e = [2; 1, 2, 1,  1, 4, 1,  1, 6, 1,  1, 8, 1,  1, 10, 1, …]

an irrational that counts while it breathes

term 0 · p/q = 2/1 · err 0.7e0

φ refuses to be approximated — its continued fraction is [1; 1, 1, 1, 1, …], nothing but ones, and ones converge slowest. π is gracious but chaotic: [3; 7, 15, 1, 292, 1, 1, 1, 2, …], the 292 lets 355/113 sneak surprisingly close. √2 is flat: [1; 2, 2, 2, …], a metronome.

e is none of these. e counts. Its continued fraction lays itself out in triplets — one, two, one · then one, four, one · then one, six, one — two soft beats flanking a louder one that grows by two each cycle, forever. It is the only irrational anyone met before they knew they were meeting one, and it shows up to the page with a meter: short – long – short, short – longer – short, short – longer-still – short.

The lower lane is the same sequence read as music: each convergent pn/qn lands above the true value, then below, then above, the arc tightening as the rationals close in. The error in the readout is in units of 10⁻ᵏ; watch how it falls when the big beat lands and how it crawls when only the small ones do.

flanking 1 (small breath) middle 2k (loud beat, k = 1, 2, 3, …) p/q > e p/q < e
