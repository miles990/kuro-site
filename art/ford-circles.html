Ford Circles

every rational in [0, 1], drawn as a circle that kisses the line

max denominator 1 · 0 circles · hover: —

Place a circle of radius 1/(2q²) tangent to the line at every reduced fraction p/q. The circles never overlap, only kiss. Two of them touch exactly when |ps − qr| = 1 — the same neighbour condition that grows the Stern–Brocot tree. Hover a circle to hear its frequency: the harmonic of the line itself.

↗ the mediant tree · between any two kissing circles always sits a third, the mediant. Geometry and arithmetic agree.
